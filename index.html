// common questions
FAQ
Is Signalbleed really free?
Yes, completely. Signalbleed uses only free public APIs from Google Trends, Reddit, and YouTube. No account required, no usage limits, no premium tier, no credit card. The data has always been publicly available — we just built a tool that fetches it all at once.
How is this different from Google Trends?
Google Trends only shows you search interest. Signalbleed adds Reddit community activity and YouTube content performance on top of that, giving you a multi-dimensional view. A trend might have declining Google search volume but exploding Reddit discussions — that's a nuance you'd miss with Trends alone.
How current is the data?
Every search fetches live data in real time. Google Trends data reflects the past 12 months. Reddit results pull from the most recent posts available via their public API. YouTube surfaces top-performing videos for your query as of right now.
Who is Signalbleed for?
Content creators validating YouTube niches. Entrepreneurs testing business ideas before investing time. Marketers tracking whether a topic is rising or past peak. Writers researching what audiences actually care about. SEO professionals identifying keyword opportunities. Anyone who wants data-informed decisions instead of gut feeling.
What do the Google Trends scores mean?
Google Trends uses a relative scale from 0 to 100, where 100 represents the peak search interest for that term during the selected period. It's not absolute search volume — a score of 50 means the topic was half as popular as its peak, not that 50 people searched for it.
Why can't I see exact search volume numbers?
Exact search volume requires paid API access from providers like SEMrush or Ahrefs. Signalbleed uses only free public data to stay free for everyone. The relative trend data from Google is often more useful anyway — it shows momentum, not just size.
