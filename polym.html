Polymarket has hundreds of active prediction markets across politics, crypto, sports, economics, and more. The problem is finding the right ones fast — especially when a news event breaks and you need to move quickly.

Signalbleed is a free search tool built on top of Polymarket's public API. Type any topic and instantly see every relevant open market: the current odds, total volume traded, available liquidity, and resolution date. All the information you need to evaluate a position, without clicking through Polymarket's interface one market at a time.

No account required. No sign-up. Signalbleed is a research layer — you still trade directly on Polymarket.

FAQ
Do I need a Polymarket account to use Signalbleed?
No. Signalbleed is a read-only research tool. You can search and browse markets without any account. To actually trade, you'll click through to Polymarket directly, where you'll need an account and USDC on Polygon.
How current are the odds and volume data?
Every search fetches live data from Polymarket's Gamma API in real time. The odds, volume, and liquidity figures reflect the current state of each market at the moment you search.
What does the probability bar represent?
The probability bar shows the current implied YES probability. In prediction markets, price equals probability — a YES price of 72¢ means the market collectively believes there's a 72% chance the event occurs.
Why do some results seem unrelated to my search?
Polymarket's search API can return loosely matched results. Signalbleed applies a client-side relevance filter that scores each market against your search terms and drops results that don't contain meaningful keyword overlap. If you see irrelevant results, try a more specific search term.
What are the edge modules coming soon?
The plan is to layer additional signal data alongside each market — news velocity, Reddit discussion activity, and price movement history — to help surface cases where public information hasn't yet been priced into a market.
Is this affiliated with Polymarket?
No. Signalbleed is an independent tool that uses Polymarket's public API. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Polymarket in any way.
